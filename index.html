About Pizza King

I'm not a chef or a professional food critic, just someone who genuinely loves pizza and has spent a long time paying attention to it. Over the years, pizza has become something I seek out wherever I go, and this site is a place to collect those experiences, opinions, and ongoing debates (NY vs CA included).

What I've learned along the way is that pizza has a unique way of bringing people together. It shows up at celebrations, late nights, family dinners, and casual hangouts. Some of the happiest moments I've seen revolve around a good pie. I've also learned that great pizza isn't limited to one city or one style; you can find it almost anywhere if you're willing to look.

I rate pizzas on a 1–10 scale, considering things like dough, balance, execution, and overall experience. Pizzas that score 7.5 or higher earn the title "Knighted by the Pizza King."

This isn't about declaring a single best pizza. It's about understanding who each slice is for, finding great pizza wherever it exists, and connecting with the pizza community along the way.

Follow along as I keep eating, traveling, and refining my taste, one slice at a time.

How I Score Pizza (1-10 Scale)

9.0-10.0: Exceptional. World-class pizza that sets the standard.

Pizza Types Guide

New York Style

Large, hand-tossed thin crust with a crispy-yet-foldable texture. Typically sold by the slice. The reference point for American pizza. Known for its wide slices, light char, and balance of sauce and cheese.

Neapolitan

The original pizza from Naples, Italy. Soft, pillowy crust with a slightly charred, leopard-spotted bottom from a wood-fired oven. Simple ingredients: San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, basil, olive oil. Cooked at extremely high heat (800°F+) for 60-90 seconds.

Detroit Style

Rectangular, thick-crusted pizza baked in a well-oiled pan, creating a crispy, caramelized cheese edge. Sauce is often applied on top of the cheese. Known for its airy, focaccia-like interior and crunchy exterior.

Chicago Deep Dish

A thick, pie-like pizza with a buttery crust that rises up the sides of a deep pan. Layered with cheese, toppings, and chunky tomato sauce on top. More casserole than flatbread—requires a fork and knife.

Roman / Al Taglio

Rectangular pizza baked in large sheets and sold by weight ("al taglio" means "by the cut"). Crispy bottom, light and airy interior, often with creative topping combinations. Designed for grab-and-go eating.

Grandma Style

Thin, rectangular pizza cooked in an olive oil-coated pan. Similar to Sicilian but thinner and crispier. Typically topped with mozzarella and sauce, sometimes in reverse order. Homestyle and comforting.
